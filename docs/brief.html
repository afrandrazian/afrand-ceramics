You keep selling on Etsy as usual. Your personal website automatically shows all the same products. When something sells on either platform, inventory updates everywhere. You also get to collect customer emails and build your own brand — something Etsy doesn't let you do today.

2. How the Pieces Fit Together
Dimension With API + Own Site Etsy Only Manual / Separate
Inventory Auto-sync both ways — sell on one, updates both Single source, simple but limited You update both manually, risk overselling
Checkout Etsy checkout + Stripe on website (2 revenue channels) Etsy only (~10-12% in fees per sale) Same two channels but inventory can get out of sync
Shipping Pirateship / manual — same either way Same Same
Marketing Two storefronts = two ways customers find you, same products Only Etsy search + Etsy ads Two stores but split / duplicated inventory headache
Customer Retention Own website = collect emails, build your brand, direct relationship Etsy owns the customer — you can't contact them directly Same potential but harder to maintain consistently
3. Why Not Shopify / Squarespace / WooCommerce?

We looked at these alternatives carefully. Here's what we found:

Approach Monthly Cost What You Get What You Lose
Our approach $0-12/mo Full control, your brand, lowest fees Needs a developer friend to set up
Shopify + Etsy sync $38-58/mo Easy to use, built-in features Monthly cost adds up, still Shopify's platform
Squarespace + Etsy sync $58-62/mo Beautiful templates Most expensive, sync plugin costs extra
WooCommerce + Etsy sync $31-46/mo Flexible, WordPress-based Most technical, needs hosting + maintenance
Etsy only (status quo) ~10-12% per sale Zero effort, what you have now No brand, no email list, Etsy controls everything
Our approach works great while you have developer help. If that ever changes, Shopify ($38/mo) is the easiest self-service option to migrate to.
These prices were researched in March 2026. Platforms change pricing — your Claude Code can help verify current rates.
